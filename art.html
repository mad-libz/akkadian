Stele of Naram-Sin

This famous limestone stele commemorates the victory of King Naram-Sin over the Lullubi people. It is celebrated for its detailed depiction of the king and his army ascending a mountain, showcasing the Akkadian emphasis on military power and divine kingship.

Head of an Akkadian Ruler

Often identified as the head of Sargon of Akkad, this bronze sculpture is renowned for its realistic detail and artistic sophistication. It reflects the high level of metallurgical skill achieved by Akkadian artists.

Cylinder Seals

These small, cylindrical objects were intricately carved with scenes of mythology, rituals, and everyday life. They were used to roll an impression onto wet clay, serving both artistic and functional purposes.

Disk of Enheduanna

A limestone disk that depicts Enheduanna, the high priestess of the moon god Nanna and daughter of Sargon of Akkad. This artifact is significant not only for its artistic merit but also because Enheduanna is one of the earliest known authors in history.
